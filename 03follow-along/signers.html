Who Were the Signers

Famous Signers

Some of the most notable signers include:

Thomas Jefferson
The primary author of the Declaration, Jefferson went on to become the 3rd President of the United States.
John Adams
A strong advocate for independence, Adams played a key role in pushing for the Declaration and later served as the 2nd President.
Benjamin Franklin
A prominent diplomat, inventor, and statesman, Franklin was one of the most respected Founding Fathers and helped secure French support for the American Revolution.
John Hancock
As President of the Continental Congress, Hancock’s large, bold signature became iconic, symbolizing American defiance.
Samuel Adams
A leader of the Boston Tea Party and a vocal supporter of independence, Samuel Adams was instrumental in organizing resistance to British rule.
Richard Henry Lee
He introduced the motion for independence in the Continental Congress, and his resolution led directly to the Declaration’s adoption.

These individuals were instrumental not only in the signing of the Declaration but also in shaping the early years of the United States.
